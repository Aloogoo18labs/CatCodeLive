      About CatCodeLive

      CatCodeLive is the web interface for JupiterScan – a trend pulse scanner on EVM. Submit momentum signals (pulses), aggregate confidence, and claim scan rewards. Stay ahead of moves.

      Connect your contract address and (optionally) your wallet to submit pulses, register as a scanner with stake, deposit fees, and claim rewards for confirmed pulses.

      Contract methods used

      • getSnapshot() – pulse count, slot count, fees, rewards, balance, paused
      • getPulse(id) – scanner, trendHash, magnitude, slotIndex, submitBlock, confirmed, rejected, confidenceScore, confirmBlock
      • getSlot(index) – startBlock, endBlock, pulseCount, totalMagnitude, winningMagnitude, closed
      • submitPulse(trendHash, magnitude, slotIndex) – submit a trend pulse (requires wallet)
      • registerScanner() – payable; stake to register as scanner
      • claimReward(pulseId) – claim reward for a confirmed pulse
      • depositFee(pulseId) – payable; attach fee to a pulse
      • getScanner(addr) – stake, totalPulses, confirmedPulses, lastSubmitBlock, banned, totalRewardsClaimed
      • getRewardForPulse(pulseId) – claimable reward amount
      • getGlobalStats() – totalPulses, confirmedPulses, rejectedPulses, pendingPulses, totalSlots, totalFees, totalRewards

      Trend categories

      Use trend strings like trend.defi, trend.nft, trend.meme, trend.gaming, or trend.other when submitting pulses (hashed on-chain).

      Networks

      Use the RPC URL field to point to any EVM chain where JupiterScan is deployed (Ethereum, Polygon, BSC, Arbitrum, Base, etc.).

      How to use CatCodeLive

      1. Enter your JupiterScan contract address in the header (0x followed by 40 hex characters).
      2. Optionally set a custom RPC URL if you are not on Ethereum mainnet.
      3. Click Connect to load the contract state: pulse count, slots, fees, rewards, and balance.
      4. To submit pulses or claim rewards, click Connect wallet and approve the connection in MetaMask (or your wallet).
      5. On the Dashboard tab you see the main stats and a global stats table.
      6. On the Pulses tab you can load pages of pulses by page number.
      7. On the Slots tab you see all slots with their block ranges and pulse counts.
      8. On the Scanner tab enter any address to see their scanner profile (stake, pulses, confirmed, banned, rewards claimed). The leaderboard shows top scanners by confirmed pulses.
      9. On the Claim tab click Refresh claimable to list your claimable rewards; then click Claim next to each to send the claim transaction.
      10. On the Actions tab you can register as a scanner (stake minimum 0.05 ETH) or deposit an optional fee to a pulse by ID.

      Submit pulse form

      Trend: any string (e.g. trend.defi). It will be hashed with Keccak256 on submit. Magnitude: number in ETH units (e.g. 1e16 for 0.01 ETH scale). Slot: the slot index (use 0 or the current slot from the contract).

      Security notes

      Never share your private key. CatCodeLive runs in your browser and only sends transactions when you click actions (Submit, Claim, Register, Deposit). Contract address and RPC URL are stored in localStorage for convenience.

      JupiterScan contract constants (reference)

      Slot duration: 6471 blocks. Min scanner stake: 0.05 ETH. Reward claim window: 4032 blocks after confirmation. Cooldown between submissions: 12 blocks. Max pulse magnitude: 1e18. Protocol version: 304.

      API compatibility

      CatCodeLive can be driven from the console: use window.CatCodeLive to access connectContract, connectWallet, loadPulses, loadSlots, fillGlobalStatsTable, refreshClaimable, exportSnapshotJson, downloadText, startAutoRefresh, stopAutoRefresh, getCurrentSlotFromContract, formatEth, weiToEth, shortAddr, validateAddress, parseMagnitude, showToast. Example: CatCodeLive.exportSnapshotJson().then(CatCodeLive.downloadText.bind(null, 'snapshot.json')).
